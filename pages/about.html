About Easy Link

Connecting Arua, One Hotspot at a Time

What is Easy Link?

Easy Link is a community-focused Wi-Fi hotspot provider based in Arua City, Uganda. We are dedicated to bridging the digital divide by offering reliable and affordable internet access to residents, students, businesses, and visitors. Our network utilizes strategically placed hotspots, allowing users to connect easily using prepaid vouchers available from local agents and shops.

We believe that access to information and communication is a fundamental need in today's world. Easy Link aims to empower the Arua community by making the internet accessible and affordable for everyone, fostering education, business growth, and social connection.

Our Mission

To provide affordable, reliable, and community-first Wi-Fi access throughout Arua City, ensuring everyone has the opportunity to connect, learn, and grow.

Our Vision

To expand quality internet access across Northern Uganda, becoming a leading force in digital inclusion and community empowerment through connectivity.

Our Story

Easy Link was founded by Emmanuel Wani Bongo Julius, a passionate entrepreneur with a deep understanding of the connectivity challenges faced by the Arua community. Witnessing the high costs and limited availability of reliable internet, Emmanuel envisioned a solution that was both affordable and accessible to the average person.

Starting with a single hotspot near Arua Primary School, Easy Link was born out of a desire to make a tangible difference. The focus was simple: create a user-friendly system using vouchers that local shops could easily sell, making internet access as common as buying airtime. Through dedication and community support, Easy Link has steadily grown its network and user base, driven by the core belief that connectivity fuels progress.

Today, Easy Link continues to expand, guided by its founding principles of affordability, reliability, and community focus. We are proud to be a local business serving the needs of Arua and look forward to connecting more communities in Northern Uganda.
